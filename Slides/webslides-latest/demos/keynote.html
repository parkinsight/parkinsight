An Android App to track Parkinson's patient and medication performance

ParkInsight is an open source app that tracks the effect of Parkinson's Disease on a daily basis using sensor data and machine learning.

Context

  • Motor Symptoms

    Tremors, Rigidity, Bradykinesia, Instability.

  • Diagnosis and tracking

    MDS-UPDRS Scale is used.

  • Medical Visits

    Are scheduled every six months.

  • 0.2% People

    Are diagnosed with Parkinson's Disease in Canada.

  • $11k+ Health System Cost

    For a PD Patient in Canada.

  • 10+ Million

    Patients around the world.

ParkInsight was made to help patients and researchers.

We talked to patients for feedback on our concept, and reached out to research organisations for collaboration.

Patients wanted constructive feedback, instead of an arbitrary number representing their performance.

Most PD medications have a very limited window of effectiveness, requiring the patients to take the dose in a specific few hours for the medication to be effective.

We decided to change our design to suit their needs better. While the backend and MVP implementation stays the same, design steps need to be taken to abstract the raw score from the user and display performance improvements with changes in Diet, Medication and Sleep routines.

Open-Source Collaboration and Data Sharing.

ParkInsight has been approved for data use by Michael J. Fox Foundation's Parkinson's Progression Marker's Intiative (PPMI) and The National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke (NINDS) Parkinson's Disease Biomarkers Program (PDBP).

We are reaching out to more organisations and researchers. Data collected by ParkInsight will be shared openly to further research efforts.

Challenges

We ran into a few roadblocks along the way.

The approval process for existing databases is tedious, and took longer than expected.

Getting access to raw sensor data from research studies was a challenge. Most available data is processed and difficult to use for training.

It's difficult to extract similar processed features from our device and conform to varying data formats in research studies.

Validation of the model is difficult without a large sample size.
